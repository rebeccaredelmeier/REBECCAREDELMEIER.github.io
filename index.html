About

Rebecca Redelmeier is a journalist based in Brooklyn. Her reporting has been published by the Globe and Mail, The Verge/Vox, Undark, Daily Maverick, and other outlets in the U.S., Canada, and overseas. She has also contributed data visualizations and analyses to the Committee to Protect Journalists and NewsWhip. Currently, she is the newsroom intern at New York Public Radio. Her stories have aired on WNYC's All Things Considered and Morning Edition, and her reporting, photos, and data visualizations have been published on Gothamist.com.

Redelmeier is a Lorana Sullivan Foundation Scholar at the Craig Newmark School of Journalism at CUNY, where she is pursuing a Master's of Arts in journalism with a focus on investigative reporting. Previously, she was the audience engagement associate at the Committee to Protect Journalists. There, she connected audiences with reporting and research on global press freedom and reported on select press freedom issues.

Redelmeier graduated from Tufts University in 2019 with a B.A. in English and a computer science minor. She grew up in Toronto, Canada, and has also lived and reported in Cape Town, South Africa. She is a 2019 recipient of the Overseas Press Club Foundation fellowship for her reporting on the transgender community in Cape Town.
